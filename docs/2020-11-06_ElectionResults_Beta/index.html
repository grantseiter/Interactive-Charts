County Level Election Results: COVID-19 Deaths and Cases vs. Margin Shift

(Hover for additional data)

Notes:
- 'Margin Shift' equals the difference between the electoral margin-of-victory (MOV) in 2020 and in 2016. A positive margin shift represents a shift in votes cast for the Republican candidate and a negative margin shift represents a shift in votes cast for the Democratic candidate. MOV is the difference between the share of votes cast for the winning candidate and the second-place chandidate in an election. For example, if Joe Biden wins the election in Fairfax County, Virginia with 70 percent of the vote and Donald Trump, the second-place finisher, wins 28 percent of the vote, Joe Biden's MOV is equal to - 42. If in 2016, Hillary Clinton won in Fairfax with MOV equal to -36, then the margin shift for Fairfax County would be equal to - 6.
- 'Rep Hold' == A Republican won the county in both 2020 and 2016.
- 'Dem Hold' == A Democrat won the county in both 2020 and 2016.
- 'Flip to Dem' == A Republican won the county in 2016 and a Democrat won in 2020.
- 'Flip to Rep' == A Democrat won the county in both 2016 and a Republican won in 2020.

Where Did Republicans and Democrats Experience the Largest Gains Over 2016?

Notes:
- A large margin shift is considered to be greater than 7.5 or less than -7.5.
- In 2,557 counties, the Republican candidate won in both 2016 and 2020. 1,516 of those counties strengthened their Republican majority in 2020 and 1,041 became more Democratic. In 466 counties, the Democratic candidate won in both 2016 and 2020. 319 of those counties strengthened their Democratic majority in 2020 and 147 became more Republican.
